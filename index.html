About us

This project presents an interactive web-based dashboard that visualizes speeding fines data across Australian states and territories in 2023. Its core aim is to convert large volumes of enforcement records into a clear, accessible, and engaging format to support public understanding and informed decision-making.

Target

  • Young people (18-25): More likely to seek information online—making them a key audience for this web-based speeding overview.
  • Mature drivers (40–64): Encouraged to reflect on behavior using relatable insights.
  • Policymakers & Law Enforcement: Use data to inform enforcement strategy.
  • General Public & Educators: Raise awareness and aid driver safety education.
  • Journalists & Media: Extract data-driven stories and visualize key trends.

Purpose

  • Identify which states issue the most fines
  • Compare enforcement methods like speed cameras or police patrols
  • Explore age groups most affected by fines
  • Recognize time-based or region-specific trends

Impact

  • Visual clarity: Makes complex data easy to understand
  • Behavioral insight: Encourages accountability in high-risk demographics
  • Strategic value: Assists in evidence-based policy decisions
  • Media & educational utility: Powers storytelling with clear data

Australian Speeding Fines Explorer – 2023

  • More than 2.7 million speeding fines were issued nationally in 2023.
  • VIC and NSW recorded the highest fine volumes.
  • Mature drivers (40–64) had higher proportions of camera-detected violations.
  • Speed camera use rose 18% from pre-pandemic levels (compared to 2022).
